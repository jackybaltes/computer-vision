Introduction

The goal of this assignment is to develop a magic trick that can be performed via the Internet.

A user draws one of the Zener symbols on a notepad. The Zener symbols include circle, plus, wave, square, and star.

The computer correctly predicts the drawn symbol and then verifies it by having the user show the drawn symbol to the screen.

A video showing the magic trick is shown below.

Methodology

The assignment can be broken down into three main parts: training data, pose estimation, optical flow, classification, and revelation.

Training Data

First record at least 5 subjects drawing the various Zener symbols. Your videos should look similar to the ones below.

Pose Estimation

You can use Mediapipe, Movenet, or Pose Detection to find the position of the head and shoulders of the user.

Extract optical flow or other movement of feature points

Implement a routine that can extract the optical flow from these regions of interest from a video sequence of the user drawing one of the symbols.

Note that in addition to the optical flow, the timing may also be an important indicator of what the user has drawn.

Classifier

Convert the information from the optical flow into a tensor (multi-dimensional feature vector), that can be used to train a support vector machine (SVM) or neural network to distinguish between the different symbols.

Revelation

After the computer has made its prediction, the user will reveal what they have drawn by holding it in front of the camera.

OpenCV

The OpenCV library is a powerful computer vision library with language bindings for python, java, javascript, and C++. The library includes many high performance implementation of simple and advanced vision algorithms. You can use this library when implementing a solution to This assignment.

Machine Learning

There are many libraries that are popular for machine learning: scikit-learn, PyTorch, TensorFlow, and Keras. You can pick one of those or any similar library for your project.

Hint

Magicians know for centuries that most people will choose the star (followed by the wave) when asked to select a random Zener symbol. So if your algorithm is unsure, you could always guess star.
